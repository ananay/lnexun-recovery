Shazam — First Hacking Bug Bounty!

So it was just the end of my Preboard Exams and of course, I wanted to catch a break. There are a lotta things I do in my free time, and one of them is of course “hacking”. For those who don’t know, I’m a white hat hacker, which means I hack for good (which doesn’t involve any kind of emails or accounts).

I often grab hold of any random app on my phone or on my laptop and start by inspecting all the processes that take place. This time, it was “Shazam”. Shazam’s an app that recognizes music which plays in the background and gives you the name. I always have this app on my Mac, because I’m a diehard fan of music and sometimes there’s a song in a video or maybe a concert that I don’t know.

Coming back to the hacking part, I first saw all the servers to which Shazam was communicating with. Now obviously, to break in I had to find a vulnerability. The vulnerability that I found in Shazam’s servers was a pretty common one, named “HeartBleed”. This was a pretty serious issue that I reported to Shazam.

A couple days later, I got a reply from them thanking me for reporting the bug, as well as a gift of $500 for responsible disclosure. That being said, I must say that it was really generous of them for rewarding me. Thank you Shazam! :D

Bug Bounty Timeline:

January 24, 2017 — Initial Bug Report
January 26, 2017 — Bug Acknowledged and fixed
February 4, 2017 — Bounty awarded!

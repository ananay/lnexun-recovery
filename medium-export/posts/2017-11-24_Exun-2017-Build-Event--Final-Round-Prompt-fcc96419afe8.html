Exun 2017 Build Event: Final Round Prompt

Introduction

You’re “Antariksh”, a Space Exploration company and you’ve acquired the rights to a new habitable planet discovered. You’re required to launch a new program which onboards people from earth to the new planet. The website/video/graphics should convince normal human beings to sign up for the migration. You can come up with a name for the planet. Make sure you notify people of the risk without it being an obstacle for the onboarding process.

What is my task?

For the website,

You need to create a landing page. You can add other informational pages to better describe the mission and sell it to the people. Try to include assets from AV and/or Design. Goal is to convince anyone visiting the website to sign up for the migration and provide their email address to successfully subscribe.

For the AV,

Make an Advert which goes on to tell people why they should move to this new planet and how it is better than the earth. Again, the end goal is to make them sign up for it by sending them to the website (or invent a smarter way to get their information).

For Design,

Create digital marketing assets for Antariksh and this migration mission.

What am I being judged on?

  • Originality: The more imaginative the better.
  • Creativity: Don’t be afraid of letting your imagination flow. There are absolutely no restrictions as to the content and arrangement of information.
  • Aesthetic Appeal: No one likes pink cursive text against a black background. Please design something that is good to look at.
  • Utilization of Resources: How good you are at what you used to make it.

How much can I take from the internet?

You may use the internet for stock images and stock videos. Using templates and copying designs defeats the purpose and is prohibited. However, you may cite the source of the design in the bibliography if you have to absolutely use it.

How do I begin?

  • What is the first thing that comes to my mind when I think of this?
  • What would I want from such a product (or service) if I owned it?
  • What would be the unique features of this product (or service)?
  • What all could I do with it?

Based on the answers you get to these questions, write a small description of what you think the product (or service) is all about. Most importantly, don’t let these questions limit your submission.

What software can I use?

  • Design: Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Sketch
  • Web Development: Sublime Text, Atom, jQuery and JS plugins, PHP, Node.js, Prepros
  • Motion Graphics: Adobe After Effects

If you wish to use a software program not listed above, please notify us and we will let you know if you can use it to make your submission.

How do I submit?

  • Compress your project files (or source code) into a single file. Set its filename to your name, school, and, most importantly, the name of your event. For instance, if your name is John Doe, you’re from Delhi Public School, R. K. Puram, your filename should be “johndoe_dpsrkp_build”.
  • All submissions must contain a readme.txt file with information about the software used to create the submission. If there is anything you would like to tell us about your submission, you may do so in the text file.

Anything I need to keep in mind before submitting?

  • Students submitting websites can send their URL of their submission. However, we also require that you send us all your source files in a zip folder. We will view your websites locally.
  • Students submitting a user-interface design should save their JPEGs/PNGs of their submission. Also, you must send us all your source files (Photoshop, Illustrator, or Sketch files) in a zip folder.

Good luck!
